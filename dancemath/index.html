What is the Universal Unit System?

The Universal Unit System (UUS) is a method created by dance legend Skippy Blair for "reading" and analyzing dance rhythms like how a musician reads sheet music.

The "Unit"

A unit is a two-beat increment representing weight change and movement in dance. Combining these increments like words will create a sentence (a dance pattern). Understanding these units wil let you string them together to create any complex dance pattern.

"Sound" vs. "Silence"

The UUS simplifies dance into two states: Sound and Silence. To a dancer, "rhythm" is the arrangement of weight changes (steps) over time. Sound (â€¢): You take a step (a weight change). Silence (/): You pause or hold (no weight change).

The Rolling Count

Blair argued that counting count "1, 2, 3, 4" makes dancers "plod" or march because they only move exactly when the number is spoken. The Rolling Count inserts sounds between the beats to keep the body moving continuously:

    "& a 1, & a 2"
  • The "&": This is when your Center Point of Balance (CPB) moves. Your body starts moving before your foot steps.
  • The "a": This is when your foot releases from the floor.
  • The "1": This is when your foot strikes the floor (the beat).
    • The Rolling Count makes movement continuous like a wave rather than robotic like a ticking clock.
